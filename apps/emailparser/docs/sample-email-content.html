Subject: Target ZERO Tips Tuesday

 

Good afternoon, everyone,

 

April is National Distracted Driving Awareness Month!

 

This month is a perfect opportunity to focus on one of the most preventable risks on our roads. Distracted driving isn’t just about phones; it includes anything that takes your hands, eyes, or mind off the task of driving. Adjusting the radio, eating, reaching for something in the backseat, or even just zoning out are all distractions that can lead to serious consequences. Every day, each of us plays a role in building a safer transportation system. Whether you work behind a desk, on the road, in planning, or in public outreach, your choices and conversations contribute to a broader cultural shift that saves lives!

 

Raising Internal Awareness: No matter what office you officially call ‘home,’ we all can play a role in supporting safety

  • Print the attached flyer and hang it in your office, cubicle, or near your workstation. A quick visual reminder can spark a moment of reflection and reinforce that focused driving is part of the culture we’re building.
  • Use the attached Safety Moment slides during meetings or team check-ins. They include a trivia question and discussion points to help coworkers think beyond cell phones and recognize all the forms of distraction we face behind the wheel.

 

In the Community: Grass-roots outreach efforts greatly improve safety messaging effectiveness – these one-on-one conversations have far-reaching impacts

  • Whether you’re chatting with a neighbor, catching up with a friend, or volunteering in your community, distracted driving is a topic that’s always worth bringing up. It’s not just about phones; distractions come in many forms, like multitasking, daydreaming, or even driving while tired. Sharing these reminders in everyday conversations helps people see that staying focused is something we can all work on.
  • Use our “Drive Time is You Time” video to spark conversations about the importance of staying focused behind the wheel. Share it with your community to help others see that safe driving is something we all benefit from and all have a role in.

 

In Your Everyday Life: Even when you’re not ‘on-the-clock,’ there are ways to improve safety culture in Florida

  • Let others see you put distractions aside when you drive. Put your phone away, finish meals before getting behind the wheel, and avoid multitasking on the road. Setting a strong example encourages others to do the same, even if they don’t say it out loud.
  • Use the attached trivia cards to test your safety knowledge with friends and family. These cards highlight the everyday distractions we all face and are part of a growing “Safety Pursuit” game designed to keep all of us learning!

 

Whether you’re reviewing plans, attending meetings, responding to emergencies, or simply commuting to and from work, your awareness and actions matter. Florida’s Target Zero initiative is built on the belief that fatalities and serious injuries are preventable, not inevitable, and it’s going to take all of us to reach that goal. Thank you for being part of this mission!
